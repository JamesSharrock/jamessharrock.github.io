About me



Hey, I'm James Sharrock. I'm a mathmatician, computer scientist and artificial intelligence enthusiast. I'm currently taking my A-Level course at North Halifax Grammar School studying Maths, Further Maths, Computer Science and Physics. I'm also studying an Extended Project Qualification where I attempt to answer, 'Can Artificial Intelligence Simulate Human Intelligence?'. I have taken extensive research in the subject focusing specifically on the Turing Test and John Searle's Chinese Room Argument.

I currently program in Python and HTML although I have some experience in JavaScript and as my A-Level Computer Science Programming Project I am recreating the 1969 Chicago Coin Speedway arcade game. This is ideal for me as I have a past game to base my creation on giving me a criteria for success.

I have work experience at one of the UK's largest engineering solutions companies where I shadowed engineers, using AutoCAD software to perform revisions of blueprints for a section of a new smart motorway. I then further volunteered with the company in the summer of 2019, maintaining a good relationship with the company. I continue to have a number of references at the company.

I have an entrepreneurial spirit and I was in the Valish Young Enterprise Team of 2020 where we came 2nd in the North West. As a team we developed eco-friendly solutions to car air fresheners reducing waste through a reusable wooden template and allowing for personalised scents. I also entered the UK Youth Rocketry Competition with a team where we had plans to uphold the title held by our predecessors who won the competition in 2019. Unfortunately due to Covid-19 this was cancelled.

In my spare time I enjoy kayaking, cycling, walking and going to the gym. I have a blue belt in taekwondo and my proficiency in swimming as well as a life-saving skills qualification. Being active and maintaining a good diet is important to me and I enjoy cooking. I am passionate about Formula One and support the Mclaren constructors team.

This website was created to store my projects and notes from my Mathematics and Computer Science Studies and acts as a portfolio of my work. Here I include my past projects such as my GCSE and A-Level programming projects as well as projects I'm currently working on such as my EPQ. I hope you enjoy my work and any feedback is welcome.

James
